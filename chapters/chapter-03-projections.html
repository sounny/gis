The Earth is approximately spherical ‚Äî yet every map you've ever used is flat. This seemingly simple transformation introduces distortions that affect how we perceive area, shape, distance, and direction.

Geodesy

The science of accurately measuring and representing the Earth's shape, gravitational field, and rotation. Modern geodesy relies on satellites, GPS, and precise mathematical models.

üìç

Datum

A mathematical model of the Earth used as a reference for coordinates. Common datums include WGS84 (used by GPS), NAD83 (North America), and NAVD88 (vertical datum).

Map Projection

A systematic transformation of the Earth's 3D surface onto a 2D plane. Every projection preserves some properties (area, shape, distance, direction) while distorting others.

üìè

Coordinate System

Geographic coordinates use latitude/longitude on a sphere. Projected coordinates (like UTM or State Plane) use X/Y on a flat surface with units in meters or feet.

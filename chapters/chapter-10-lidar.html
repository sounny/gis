ü¶á Seeing with Light

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) is an active remote sensing method. Similar to how a bat uses sonar (sound) to map a cave, LiDAR uses pulses of laser light to map the surface of the Earth.

A LiDAR sensor mounted on an aircraft creates a Point Cloud: millions of X, Y, Z coordinates representing everything the light hit‚Äîtrees, power lines, houses, and the ground.

Key Difference: Unlike imagery which creates pixels (2D), LiDAR creates a cloud of points (3D).

‚ö° How LiDAR Works: The Multi-Return Principle

The superpower of LiDAR is its ability to "penetrate" vegetation. It doesn't actually go through leaves, but because the laser beam has a physical width, some of the light hits top branches, while some passes through gaps to hit lower branches or the ground.

This creates roughly 3 types of returns from a single pulse:

1st Return
Hits the highest feature (Canopy Top). Used for DSM.
Intermediate Return
Hits branches/structures "inside" the canopy. Structure info.
Last Return
Hits the ground. Critical for creating the Bare Earth DEM.

üèîÔ∏è Surface Models: DEM vs. DSM vs. CHM

By filtering the point cloud, we can create three distinct 3D models. Understanding the difference is critical for spatial analysis.

DEM

Digital Elevation Model

Specifically the "Bare Earth" topography only.

DSM

Digital Surface Model

First returns: Sees tops of trees and buildings.

CHM

Canopy Height Model

Object height above ground.

The "Golden Equation" of Lidar Analysis:

DSM (Top) - DEM (Ground) = CHM (Height)

üõ†Ô∏è Applications in the Real World

Why do we need this 3D data?

  • Flood Modeling: Water flows on the DEM (ground), not the DSM (treetops). Accurate DEMs are vital for predicting flood risk.
  • Forestry (Biomass): A CHM allows us to estimate the volume of timber or carbon stored in a forest without walking every acre.
  • Archaeology: "Digital Deforestation" allows archaeologists to reveal ancient ruins (like Mayan cities) hidden under dense jungle canopy by filtering for only ground returns.

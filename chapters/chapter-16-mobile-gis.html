Summary of Big Ideas

  • Real-Time Sync: Data collected in the field appears on the office dashboard instantly.
  • Offline Maps: Modern apps allow workers to download maps to their device for use in remote areas without cell service.
  • Attribute Validation: Survey forms use "logic" to prevent errors (e.g., preventing a user from entering a negative value for tree height).
  • Crowdsourcing: Mobile GIS enables regular citizens to report issues like potholes or water leaks to the city.

Important Person

Dr. Monica Turner

A Vilas Research Professor at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, Dr. Turner is a pioneer in landscape ecology. Her long-term studies of the recovery of Yellowstone National Park after the 1988 fires were among the first to integrate field data collection with advanced GIS modeling, proving how important "real-time" observation is for understanding environmental change.

Local to Global

Austin's FloodPro Dashboard

In Austin, flooding happens fast. The City uses a mobile-ready GIS dashboard called FloodPro. During a storm, residents can use their phones to see real-time water levels from hundreds of creek sensors across the city. This data is synced instantly, allowing emergency responders to close low-water crossings before casualties occur.

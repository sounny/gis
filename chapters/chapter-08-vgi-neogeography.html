üë• What is VGI?

Volunteered Geographic Information (VGI) is the harnessing of tools to create, assemble, and disseminate geographic data provided voluntarily by individuals. Instead of relying solely on professional surveyors, we now have millions of "citizens as sensors."

The "Wikification" of GIS: Just as Wikipedia democratized knowledge, VGI has democratized map-making.

üåê OpenStreetMap (OSM)

OpenStreetMap is the greatest example of VGI. It is a free, editable map of the whole world being built by volunteers. In humanitarian crises, OSM often provides more up-to-date information than any official government source.

Summary of Big Ideas

  • Citizens as Sensors: Every smartphone user is a potential map contributor.
  • Open Data fuels innovation and provides critical info in data-poor regions.
  • NeoGeography is the use of mapping tools by non-experts for personal/social activities.
  • Data Quality in VGI is managed through "crowd-review" and automated checks.

üìö Glossary

Citizen Science:
The collection and analysis of data relating to the natural world by members of the general public.
Crowdsourcing:
Obtaining information or input into a task by enlisting the services of a large number of people.
Geotagging:
The process of adding geographical identification metadata to various media such as photos or videos.

Important Person

Dr. Michael Goodchild

A Professor Emeritus at UC Santa Barbara, Goodchild is one of the world's leading GIS scientists. In 2007, he coined the term "Volunteered Geographic Information" (VGI), predicting that the future of mapping would lie in the hands of the public rather than just professional organizations.

Local to Global

iNaturalist: Born in Texas

One of the world's most successful VGI platforms, iNaturalist, has deep roots in Texas (partnered with Texas Parks and Wildlife). Thousands of Texans use it to "volunteer" observations of biodiversity, which helps scientists track climate change and species migration in real-time.

By mapping your backyard, you are contributing to a global scientific database.

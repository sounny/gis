The Changing Face of Output

Traditionally, GIS output meant a printed paper map. Today, output is dynamic.

  • Static Maps: PDF, JPEG, PNG (Fixed scale, fixed extent).
  • Dynamic Maps: Web Maps, Dashboards (User interaction, pan/zoom).
  • Mobile Maps: Offline usage, GPS integration.

Export Formats

Raster Exports

JPEG: Good for complex imagery (photos), lossy compression.

PNG: Good for line art and flat colors, supports transparency.

TIFF: Best for archiving, supports georeferencing tags (GeoTIFF).

Vector Exports

PDF: Universal document format, preserves layers (sometimes).

SVG: Scalable Vector Graphics for web.

AI: Adobe Illustrator format for graphic design refinement.

The Politics and Ethics of Map Output

üá∫üá∏ Texas Connection: The General Land Office Map Collections

Historical Context: Texas has one of the longest cartographic records in North America. The General Land Office (GLO) maintains over 2.5 million historical maps and documents. These include Spanish colonial maps, Anglo-American surveyor plats, and modern parcel maps.

Format Challenges: The GLO faces a critical decision: How to preserve and share these maps? Some are fragile originals; others are digital scans. The agency must balance:

  • Preservation: Archival TIFF formats with metadata and georeferencing for long-term survival.
  • Access: Web-friendly formats (PNG, JPEG) to enable online exploration via the Remonter le Temps project.
  • Accuracy: Vector transcription of historical boundaries for modern property disputes and legal research.
  • Privacy: Removing sensitive owner information from public digital releases.

Global parallel: European national cadastral archives face identical challenges, especially in countries transitioning from paper to digital records.
